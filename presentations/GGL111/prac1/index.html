Basic Rock Types

  • Sedimentary
    • Bits of other rocks which are eventually cemented together
    • Moved by rivers, glaciers, oceans, wind &c
  • Igneous
    • Rocks that were liquid (and hot) and cooled down.
    • Either extrusive or intrusive
  • Metamorphic
    • Rocks that started as other rocks and have been altered by heat and/or pressure

Geological Maps

Show lithology (type of rock)

Always have a legend (somewhere)

Legends go from oldest to youngest rocks

Geological Profiles

Are a side view of a vertical 2D plane

Show the topography at surface

Show the inferred geology below surface

Creating a geological cross section (1/3)

1. Create a topographic profile:

  • Record the contours along your profile
  • Place these points on the profile
  • Link the points together smoothly

Creating a geological cross section (2/3)

2. Determine the underlying geology:

  • Record the contacts along your profile
  • Place these points on the profile
  • Link the points that are of the same contact
  • Recommendation: Do each contact to completion, then do the next one

Creating a geological cross section (3/3)

3. Add other estimates:

  • Add a title eg: "Geological Cross-Section of Map 3"
  • Include the vertical and horizontal scales
  • Include a geological legend
  • Include markers for which end of the profile is which: E, W, X, Y (whatever)
